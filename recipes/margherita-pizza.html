Margherita Pizza

Description

A Margherita pizza is a simple, classic Italian pizza characterized by its fresh, vibrant flavors and minimalist toppings. It typically features a thin crust, a bright tomato sauce (often using San Marzano tomatoes), fresh mozzarella cheese, and fresh basil leaves, all drizzled with olive oil.

The combination of these fresh ingredients creates a delicious balance of tangy, creamy, and herbaceous notes.

Ingredients

  • dough
  • tomato sauce
  • mozzarella
  • Basil
  • olive oil
  • salt

Steps

  1. Simple, iconic ingredients: Margherita pizza is defined by its simple, fresh ingredients: San Marzano tomatoes (or a similar high-quality tomato), fresh mozzarella cheese, and fresh basil leaves. These ingredients are traditionally chosen for their quality and allow each flavor to shine through.
  2. Italian Flag Colors: The red of the tomatoes, the white of the mozzarella, and the green of the basil represent the colors of the Italian flag. This connection is often cited in the popular legend of its creation for Queen Margherita of Savoy.
  3. Neapolitan Origin: Margherita pizza is a classic Neapolitan pizza, originating from Naples, Italy. Neapolitan pizza, including Margherita, is known for its light, airy, and slightly chewy crust, often baked in a wood-fired oven.
  4. Freshness is Key: The best Margherita pizzas emphasize the freshness of the ingredients. This means using fresh mozzarella cheese, ideally a buffalo mozzarella or fior di latte, and fresh basil leaves, rather than dried herbs or pre-shredded cheese.
